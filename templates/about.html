About

Nikk Ogasa grew up on the coast of southern California, where he developed a healthy fear of the open water. He spent his school breaks hooking trout and tripping on rocks in the Sierra Nevada, and fostered a fierce fascination for wild landscapes and the natural world. In university he followed this passion by majoring in geology, and studying rocks has been an integral part of his life ever since.

Geology has taken him around the globe, from the blistering sands of the Gobi desert to quiet towns in the Pyrenees mountains. He spent two weeks exploring an underground gold mine in rural Quebec (in the winter!), collecting rocks for his masters thesis work at McGill University. Although conditions were sometimes rough, Nikk loved geoscience. After all, what could compare to unearthing stories millions of years old?

Today he's still chasing stories about the natural world, with a focus on science and the environment. You can find him in Santa Cruz, California, where he's chipping away at a M.S. in Science Communication at U.C. Santa Cruz.

When he's not writing stories, losing his way in the mountains, fiddling with a Python script, or rolling around on a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu mat, he's plucking away (poorly) at a banjo.
