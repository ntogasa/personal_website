About

I'm a science writer and graduate student in the University of California Santa Cruz Science Communiation Program. At the moment I write for the Monterey Herald, and mostly report on environmental issues. My background is in rocks and earthquakes. While earning my masters degree in geology from McGill University, I studied how ancient earthquakes formed large gold deposits in Quebec. Before that I worked at the United State Geological Survey, and investigated how a geothermal energy field was actively deforming the surrounding landscape. Computer programming has been a hobby of mine for years, and I am interested in learning to apply my skills to journalism. I have experience with web scraping, data visualization, back end web design, and database management. Thanks for stopping by, feel free to reach out to me anytime.
