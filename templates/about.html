About

Nikk Ogasa grew up on the coast of southern California, where he developed a healthy fear of the open water. He spent his school breaks hooking trout and tripping on rocks in the Sierra Nevada, and grew a fierce (and perhaps obsessive) passion for wild landscapes and the natural world.

Geology has taken him around the globe, from the blistering sands of the Gobi desert to quiet towns in the Pyrenees mountains. He spent two weeks going into an underground gold mine in rural Quebec (in the winter!), hammering rocks for his masters degree at McGill University.

Now he's writing about science and the environment to share his love for the natural world. You can find him in Santa Cruz, California, where he's chasing a M.S. in Science Communication at UC Santa Cruz.

When he's not writing about the environment, losing his way in the mountains, fiddling with a Python script, or rolling around on a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu mat, he's plucking away (poorly) at a banjo.
