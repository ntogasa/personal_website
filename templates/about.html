About

Nikk grew up on the coast of southern California, where he developed a healthy fear of the open water. He spent his school breaks hooking trout and tripping on rocks in the Sierra Nevada, and fostered a fierce (and perhaps obsessive) passion for wild landscapes and the natural world.

In 2015, he wrestled a B.S. in Earth Science from the University of California Santa Cruz, and in 2020 surprised everyone (including himself) with a M.S. in Geology from McGill University. Somewhere along the way, he puzzled over earthquakes at the United States Geological Survey's Earthquake Science Center.

Now he's writing about science and the environment to share his love for the natural world. He lives in Santa Cruz, CA, where he's clawing and scratching his way towards a M.S. in Science Communication at UC Santa Cruz.

When he's not writing about science, losing his way in the mountains, fiddling with a Python script, or rolling around on a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu mat, he's plucking away clumsily at a banjo.
